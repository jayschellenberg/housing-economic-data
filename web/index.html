    Geographies

    Multi-select Provinces and CMAs; charts & tiles update live. CMA-level data exists for housing prices, CPI, construction cost, commercial leasing and building permits — not every series (national-only series like mortgage rates & bond yields always render).

    to

    Month & year. Leave blank to show all available history.

    Sections

    One sheet per section + a Metadata sheet with source URLs and units.

    Every visible chart as an image — one per page / worksheet.

    Sources: Bank of Canada Valet; Statistics Canada Web Data Service; the Office of the Superintendent of Bankruptcy Canada (insolvency statistics, via Open Government); and CMHC (average rents). Contains information licensed under the Statistics Canada Open Licence and the Open Government Licence – Canada. —

    Current Snapshot

    Latest reading per indicator with 90-day / 12-month / 24-month change chips. Tap an indicator name in the Market Indicators tab for the underlying chart.

    Green chips = favourable direction for the indicator; red = unfavourable; grey = unchanged. Direction semantics follow the catalog's goodDirection per chart. National-only indicators (mortgage rates, bond yields, SLOS) always show; multi-geo indicators respect the Geography filter on the Market Indicators tab.

    Area

    Structural type (chart + table), dwelling condition & age (period of construction). Dwelling type is published at Canada / province / CMA-CA level; condition & age at municipality (CSD) level, plus Winnipeg community areas & neighbourhood clusters — pick a province first, then an area.

    Compare with (optional)

    Adds a second area to the point-in-time charts & tables. Not used in the compare-across-years view.

    View

    Only "% needing major repairs" is fully comparable across censuses (2016/2011/2006 combine regular + minor). Age bands are rolled into common buckets for the comparison. 2006 is a summary with only a coarse before/after-1986 age split, so it's excluded from the age-mix comparison. 2011 is the voluntary NHS (less reliable for small areas); some areas lack 2011/2006 data.

    Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Population, 2006–2021. Structural type of dwelling — table 98-10-0040 (2021), with 2016/2011 from Census Profile 98-316 and 2006 from Community Profile 92-591. Dwelling condition & period of construction — table 98-10-0233 (2021), with earlier years from the Census/NHS profiles. Contains information licensed under the Statistics Canada Open Licence.

    Census period

    Sets which census the Demographics table and demographic charts reflect. The Population & Dwelling Trends table/chart always span all censuses. 2016/2011 demographics are best-effort and need the rebuilt data; Winnipeg areas are 2021 only.

    Areas

    All three appear in both tables and the comparison charts. Each can be any Manitoba geography or a Winnipeg Community Area / Cluster / Neighbourhood. Area 1 also drives the Trends chart and dwelling-type mix.

    Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Population (2006, 2011, 2016, 2021), retrieved via CensusMapper / the cancensus R package. Contains information licensed under the Statistics Canada Open Licence. Winnipeg community/cluster/neighbourhood figures are dissemination-area aggregates (2021). Demographics for 2016/2011 are best-effort — rows whose fields differ across censuses (period-of-construction, income) are omitted.

    Sources: Statistics Canada Web Data Service (retail, manufacturing, wholesale trade, farm cash receipts, CPI, employment, building permits, housing starts — contains information licensed under the Statistics Canada Open Licence); Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation; the Winnipeg Regional Real Estate Board (WRREB); and the Canadian Real Estate Association MLS® Home Price Index. Real GDP, minimum wage, and the Economic Outlook commentary are maintained manually (see r/config/economic_update_manual.json). This product is not affiliated with or endorsed by any of these organizations.
